1 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This Electrical Safety Audit Report presents a comprehensive evaluation of the electrical systems
and safety measures at Indian Bank, Ernakulam zone –Palarivattom branch. The audit was
conducted on 19/05/2026 in accordance with NEC-2023 guidelines, CEA Regulations 2023, Indian
Bank Standards and relevant Indian Standards.
The audit team has documented all observations related to Electrical safety, which are now
submitted for review to the management of M/s. Indian Bank- Ernakulam Zone, these findings are
intended to guide the organization in strengthening its Electrical Safety Framework of the
Branches and Offsite ATM’s.
Audit Dates: 19/05/2026
Location: Palarivattom branch
Audit Conducted By: M/s. Aura Veritas Engineering and Consultancy Pvt. Ltd.
1.1 Key Findings:
The overall electrical infrastructure of the branch was found to be Operational with several areas
requiring immediate attention and corrective actions to ensure compliance with electrical safety
standards and to prevent potential hazards.
1.2 Overall Assessment:
The electrical systems require Minor to Medium level improvements to meet NEC-2023 Indian
Bank Standards compliance standards and to ensure safety of personnel, equipment, and
continuity of banking operations.
This report details all observations with photographic evidence, technical measurements, and
prioritized recommendations for corrective actions.
The audit encompassed a comprehensive evaluation of electrical safety practices, including:
•

Electrical Maintenance Team One to One Interview during Audit days.

•

Examination of Electrical Equipment at premises.

•

Verification of Electrical Parameters such as Load Measurements, Earthing Measurements,
Lux levels measurements, Maximum Current ratings verification, Thermography
measurements at each panel and Distribution boards.

2 BASIC TERMINOLOGY
1. Key Electrical Engineering Terms Everyone Should Know
Understanding these terms is crucial for anyone working with electrical systems, whether
you're conducting audits, managing installations, or ensuring safety compliance.
2. Basic Electrical Quantities
•

Voltage (V): The electrical potential difference between two points. Measured in volts.

•

Current (I): The flow of electric charge. Measured in amperes (A).

•

Resistance (R): Opposition to current flow. Measured in ohms (Ω).

•

Power (P): The rate at which electrical energy is consumed or generated. Measured in
watts (W).

•

Energy (E): Total power consumed over time. Measured in kilowatt-hours (kWh).

3. Components & Devices
•

Circuit Breaker: A safety device that interrupts current flow in case of overload or fault.

•

Transformer: A device that changes voltage levels between circuits.

•

Capacitor: Stores electrical energy temporarily and releases it when needed.

•

Inductor: Stores energy in a magnetic field when current flows through it.

•

Relay: Electrically operated switch used for control and automation.

4. System Concepts
•

Single-phase vs. Three-phase: Types of AC power systems; three-phase is more efficient
for industrial use.

•

Earthing (Grounding): Connecting electrical systems to the earth to prevent shock
hazards.

•

Load: The amount of electrical power consumed by devices or systems.

•

Short Circuit: An abnormal connection causing excessive current flow, often leading to
damage or fire.

•

Overload: When a circuit carries more current than it's designed for,

5. Measurement & Safety
•

Insulation Resistance: Measures the effectiveness of insulation in preventing current
leakage.

•

Thermography: Infrared imaging used to detect hot spots in electrical systems.

•

Harmonics: Distortions in electrical waveforms that can affect equipment performance.

•

RCD (Residual Current Device): Detects leakage currents and disconnects power to
prevent electrocution.

•

Lux Level: Measurement of light intensity, relevant in lighting audits.

6. Codes & Standards
•

IS Codes: Indian Standards for fire safety and protection.

•

NEC-2024 Guidelines

•

BS EN: British/European standards for fire equipment and systems.

7 EARTHING RESISTANCE:
Earthing resistance is the resistance between the grounding electrode and the surrounding
soil. It determines how effectively fault currents, lightning surges, or leakage currents are
dissipated into the earth.

Important for all Applications:
Safety: Prevents electric shock hazards by directing fault currents safely into the ground.
Equipment Protection: Reduces the risk of damage to electrical systems during faults or
lightning strikes.
Voltage Stabilization: Helps maintain consistent voltage levels across the system.

Load Flow Analysis is a computational method used to determine the steady-state operating
conditions of an electrical power system. It evaluates how electrical power—both real (P) and
reactive (Q)—flows through the network under specific load conditions.
Important for all Applications:
•

Required by NEC 220.87 for determining existing loads before adding new equipment

•

Prevents overloads, phase imbalance, and fire hazards

•

Ensures panel boards and feeders operate within safe limits

•

Identifies peak demand periods and power wastage

•

Supports Power factor correction and efficiency improvements

9 THERMOGRAPHY
Thermal imaging is a sophisticated and non-invasive technique that utilizes infrared technology
to detect heat emissions from various objects. It converts infrared energy into a visible light
display, helping detect abnormal heat patterns caused by overloads or impending equipment
failures.
Important for all Applications:
•

Preventing arc flash, insulation failure, and fire hazard

•

Detecting hot spots in panels, breakers, and busbars

•

Identifying loose connections and phase imbalance

•

Monitoring transformer heating and motor bearings
